================================================================================
LESSON 1: LOG TRANSFORMATION FOR NON-NEGATIVE PREDICTIONS
================================================================================

STEP 1: THE CONCEPT
-------------------
Regular Linear Regression:
  Prediction = b₀ + b₁×X₁ + b₂×X₂ + ...
  Problem: This can give ANY value including negatives!

Log Transformation:
  log(Prediction) = b₀ + b₁×X₁ + b₂×X₂ + ...
  Then: Prediction = exp(log(Prediction))
  Magic: exp(anything) is ALWAYS positive!

================================================================================
STEP 2: WHAT WE TESTED (Toy Example Results)
================================================================================

Dataset: 5 samples
- Features: Temperature (°C) and Hour
- Target: Energy consumption (kWh)

Test Case:
  Temperature: 18°C
  Hour: 8 AM
  Actual Energy: 40 kWh

RESULTS:
--------
Method                          Prediction    Can Be Negative?
Regular Linear Regression       39.45 kWh     YES ❌
Log-Transformed Regression      44.47 kWh     NO ✅ (guaranteed!)

================================================================================
STEP 3: HOW LOG TRANSFORMATION WORKS (Step by Step)
================================================================================

Training Phase:
1. Original targets: [50, 80, 30, 100] kWh
2. Transform to log: [3.932, 4.394, 3.434, 4.615]
3. Train model on LOG values

Prediction Phase:
1. Model predicts in log space: 3.817
2. Convert back: exp(3.817) - 1 = 44.47 kWh
3. Result is ALWAYS positive!

Key Functions:
- np.log1p(y)  -> Converts y to log space (log(1+y))
- np.expm1(y)  -> Converts back (exp(y)-1)
